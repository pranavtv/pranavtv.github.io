Who I Am

About Me

I am a researcher in Statistics with a deep interest in both theoretical foundations and applied dimensions of the field. My academic journey has taken me through classical frequentist inference, Bayesian paradigms, and into emerging frameworks such as Neutrosophic Statistics, which extends classical probability to handle indeterminate and inconsistent information.

My work on finite mixture models explores identifiability, estimation via EM algorithms, and model selection criteria. I am equally drawn to the interface between statistics and neural networks — understanding when deep learning can be understood through a statistical lens, and vice versa.

Outside of research, I enjoy teaching statistical concepts at various levels and writing accessible material that bridges the gap between rigorous theory and practical intuition.
