Who I Am

About Me

I am Pranav T V, a PhD Scholar in Statistics at Pondicherry University, India. My research interests include Statistical Modelling, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Mixture Models, Bayesian Statistics, Neutrosophic Statistics, and Data Science. I am passionate about developing innovative statistical methodologies and applying advanced analytical techniques to address real-world problems involving uncertainty, complex data structures, and decision-making.

My work spans classical statistical theory, modern machine learning, and emerging frameworks such as Neutrosophic Statistics — which extend probability theory to handle indeterminate and inconsistent information. I am equally comfortable designing mathematical models and implementing them computationally in Python and R.

What I Study

Research Interests

My research spans theoretical statistics and its computational dimensions, with emphasis on robust inference, latent structure, and uncertainty quantification.

📊

Statistics

Classical and modern statistical theory: estimation, hypothesis testing, asymptotic inference, and high-dimensional statistics with real-world applications.

Estimation Theory Hypothesis Testing Asymptotics
🧩

Mixture Models

Finite and infinite mixture models — identifiability, EM algorithm-based estimation, Bayesian nonparametric extensions, and model selection criteria.

EM Algorithm Identifiability Clustering
🧠

Neural Networks

Statistical perspectives on deep learning: generalization bounds, implicit regularization, uncertainty quantification, and overparameterized model theory.

Deep Learning Generalization Uncertainty
⚙️

Machine Learning

Principled ML methods grounded in statistical learning theory — supervised, unsupervised, and semi-supervised approaches to real-world prediction and inference.

Statistical Learning Supervised ML Feature Engineering
🌐

Neutrosophic Statistics

Extending classical probability and statistics to handle indeterminate, incomplete, and inconsistent data — a rapidly growing framework for real uncertainty.

Indeterminacy Neutrosophic Sets Robust Inference
🎲

Bayesian Statistics

Prior elicitation, posterior computation via MCMC and variational inference, hierarchical models, and Bayesian model comparison with applied case studies.

MCMC Hierarchical Models Prior Design

Drowsiness Detection System Using Deep Learning

Master's dissertation project completed as part of the M.Sc. Statistics and Applied Mathematics programme at the Central University of Tamil Nadu (2020–2022) under the supervision of Dr. C. Vijayalakshmi. The project focused on developing a deep learning-based drowsiness detection system capable of identifying driver fatigue through facial feature analysis and eye-state monitoring.
